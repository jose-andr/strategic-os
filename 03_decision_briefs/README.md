# Decision Briefs

## Purpose

This folder contains decision-ready advice created through Strategic OS sensemaking.

Use this folder for concise briefs that clarify a decision, recommendation, trade-off or next action.

Decision briefs should help leaders make better strategic decisions.

## What belongs here

- Decision briefs
- Strategic recommendations
- Option comparisons
- Trade-off assessments
- Decision framing notes
- Recommendation summaries
- Evidence-informed advice
- Briefs created by the Sensemaking Agent

## What does not belong here

- Raw notes
- Unprocessed meeting fragments
- Full project documentation
- General reflections
- Long research dumps
- Sensitive organisational source data
- Customer-level records
- Governed datasets
- Confidential extracts

Use summaries, caveats and decision-ready synthesis instead.

## Decision brief standard

A good decision brief should make clear:

- what decision is required
- why the decision matters
- what evidence is available
- what assumptions exist
- what options were considered
- what trade-offs matter
- what recommendation is being made
- what should happen next

## Suggested naming

Use date-first filenames.

Examples:

- `2026-06-15_decision-brief_service-account-eofy-story.md`
- `2026-06-15_decision-brief_channel-strategy-next-step.md`
- `2026-06-15_decision-brief_agents-framework.md`

## Review rhythm

Review this folder monthly or before major stakeholder conversations.

Use the review to ask:

- Which briefs are still active?
- Which decisions have been made?
- Which recommendations were adopted?
- Which briefs should be updated, archived or converted into lessons?
- Which decision patterns are reusable?

## Review checklist

Before saving a decision brief, check:

- [ ] Is the decision or recommendation clear?
- [ ] Is the brief practical and concise?
- [ ] Is evidence separated from assumptions and interpretation?
- [ ] Are trade-offs visible?
- [ ] Is the next action clear?
- [ ] Is sensitive information removed or generalised?
- [ ] Does this help someone make a better strategic decision?
