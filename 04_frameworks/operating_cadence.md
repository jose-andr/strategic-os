# Operating Cadence

## Purpose

This framework defines the operating rhythm for Strategic OS.

Strategic OS is not only a repository for storing knowledge. It is a personal operating system for turning complexity, signals, evidence and relationships into clearer strategic decisions.

The operating cadence keeps the system alive.

It creates regular moments to:
- clarify priorities
- review incoming signals
- make decisions visible
- identify strategic opportunities
- maintain stakeholder alignment
- convert experience into reusable knowledge
- improve the system over time

## Core Principle

Strategic OS should be reviewed at the rhythm of the work.

Daily cadence supports focus.

Weekly cadence supports sensemaking.

Monthly cadence supports opportunity development.

Quarterly cadence supports strategic alignment and system quality.

## Daily Cadence

### Purpose

The daily cadence helps translate current complexity into immediate focus.

It should be lightweight and action-oriented.

### Key Questions

- What matters most today?
- What decisions need attention?
- What conversations or stakeholders need preparation?
- What admin, operational or follow-up tasks are at risk of slipping?
- What should be captured before it is lost?

### Recommended Activities

#### Chief of Staff Daily Briefing

Use the Chief of Staff Agent to create a short daily briefing.

The briefing should identify:
- top priorities
- key meetings
- decisions required
- stakeholder sensitivities
- follow-ups
- risks
- useful preparation notes

#### Calendar And Priority Check

Review the day’s calendar against current strategic priorities.

Look for:
- meetings that need preparation
- meetings that should be reframed
- opportunities to influence a decision
- commitments that need follow-up
- work that should be deferred or delegated

#### Admin Task Check

Review small operational tasks that could create friction if ignored.

Examples:
- emails requiring response
- meeting notes to send
- documents to update
- actions promised to others
- tasks blocking another person

#### Decision-Required Check

Identify whether anything today needs a clear decision.

Capture:
- the decision required
- the options
- what evidence is available
- what is uncertain
- who needs to be involved
- the next action

### Suggested Outputs

Daily outputs may include:
- daily briefing
- priority list
- decision note
- meeting preparation note
- stakeholder follow-up
- inbox item
- task clarification

### Storage Guidance

Store only material that has reusable value.

Use:
- `00_inbox/` for unsorted notes or signals
- `03_decision_briefs/` for decision-related material
- `06_stakeholder_patterns/` for reusable stakeholder observations
- `08_projects/` for project-specific operating notes

Do not store sensitive organisational material or raw confidential information.

## Weekly Cadence

### Purpose

The weekly cadence converts activity into sensemaking.

It helps identify what changed, what matters, what is stuck and what should be moved forward.

### Key Questions

- What signals emerged this week?
- What patterns are becoming visible?
- What decisions moved forward?
- What decisions remain unclear?
- Where is stakeholder alignment improving or deteriorating?
- What should be shipped, paused or reframed?
- What knowledge should be saved?

### Recommended Activities

#### Sensemaking Review

Review notes, meetings, conversations and project signals from the week.

Look for:
- recurring themes
- unresolved tensions
- weak signals
- emerging risks
- stakeholder concerns
- decision bottlenecks
- opportunities for reframing

Useful outputs:
- sensemaking note
- pattern summary
- issue framing
- opportunity statement
- decision brief input

#### Shipping Review

Review what moved from thinking into action.

Ask:
- What was shipped?
- What is still too abstract?
- What needs a smaller next step?
- What is blocked?
- What can be simplified?
- What should be stopped?

Useful outputs:
- shipped work log
- next-action list
- project update
- delivery risk note

#### Stakeholder Alignment Check

Review key stakeholder relationships and alignment conditions.

Ask:
- Who is aligned?
- Who is uncertain?
- Who has not been heard?
- Who may be affected by a decision?
- What concerns need to be surfaced?
- What language or framing may help?

Useful outputs:
- stakeholder pattern note
- engagement plan
- influence map
- meeting preparation note

#### Inbox Triage

Review `00_inbox/` and decide what to do with captured material.

Each item should be:
- moved
- developed
- merged
- archived
- deleted

Suggested destinations:
- `01_career/`
- `02_strategic_opportunities/`
- `03_decision_briefs/`
- `04_frameworks/`
- `05_lessons_learned/`
- `06_stakeholder_patterns/`
- `08_projects/`
- `09_thought_leadership/`
- `10_templates/`
- `11_analytics/`

### Suggested Outputs

Weekly outputs may include:
- weekly sensemaking summary
- project movement review
- stakeholder alignment note
- decision brief update
- lessons learned entry
- strategic opportunity note
- cleaned inbox

## Monthly Cadence

### Purpose

The monthly cadence turns operating experience into strategic advantage.

It helps assess whether the system is creating better decisions, stronger positioning and more strategic opportunities.

### Key Questions

- What strategic opportunities are emerging?
- What evidence has been created?
- What work strengthened the desired reputation?
- What patterns are worth formalising?
- What templates or frameworks should be improved?
- What should be made more visible?
- What should be retired?

### Recommended Activities

#### Career Architect Opportunity Review

Review career evidence, positioning and opportunity signals.

Ask:
- What work demonstrates strategic value?
- What examples support the desired reputation?
- What capability is becoming more visible?
- What opportunity should be pursued?
- What gap should be addressed next?

Useful outputs:
- career evidence note
- capability narrative
- opportunity assessment
- portfolio example
- interview story
- positioning update

#### Strategic Opportunities Review

Review `02_strategic_opportunities/`.

Assess:
- new opportunities
- active opportunities
- weak signals
- partnership possibilities
- thought leadership openings
- roles or projects worth exploring
- opportunities to increase decision influence

Useful outputs:
- opportunity brief
- opportunity pipeline update
- next-step recommendation
- relationship action

#### Lessons Learned Review

Review recent work and extract reusable lessons.

Focus on:
- what worked
- what failed
- what surprised you
- what should be repeated
- what should be avoided
- what principle can be reused

Useful outputs:
- lesson learned entry
- reusable heuristic
- decision principle
- facilitation pattern
- stakeholder pattern

#### Frameworks And Templates Review

Review `04_frameworks/` and `10_templates/`.

Ask:
- Which frameworks were used?
- Which templates saved time?
- Which files are unclear?
- Which patterns should be standardised?
- Which tools should be simplified?
- What should become a reusable prompt?

Useful outputs:
- updated framework
- new template
- retired template
- prompt pattern
- improved operating guidance

### Suggested Outputs

Monthly outputs may include:
- strategic opportunity review
- career evidence update
- lessons learned summary
- framework improvement
- reusable template
- thought leadership seed
- decision-quality reflection

## Quarterly Cadence

### Purpose

The quarterly cadence reviews whether Strategic OS is still aligned to its North Star.

It is a deeper system review focused on quality, direction, usefulness and strategic outcomes.

### Key Questions

- Is Strategic OS helping create better strategic decisions?
- Is it increasing strategic opportunities?
- Is the repository still clean and useful?
- Are the agents performing useful roles?
- Are the frameworks still relevant?
- Is the system producing evidence of value?
- What should change next quarter?

### Recommended Activities

#### Strategic OS QA Assessment

Review the system as a whole.

Assess:
- repository clarity
- folder hygiene
- duplication
- outdated material
- missing files
- confusing structures
- whether files remain durable and reusable

Useful outputs:
- QA assessment
- clean-up list
- repository improvement backlog

#### Agent Performance Review

Review AI agent usefulness.

Assess each active agent against:
- clarity of role
- quality of outputs
- usefulness to decisions
- failure modes
- human-in-the-loop safeguards
- whether the agent should continue, change or be retired

Useful outputs:
- agent review note
- agent spec update
- prompt improvement
- new agent requirement
- retired agent note

#### Career Evidence Review

Review accumulated evidence of strategic value.

Look for:
- decisions influenced
- systems clarified
- stakeholders aligned
- projects improved
- risks reduced
- opportunities created
- reusable frameworks developed

Useful outputs:
- career evidence register
- achievement narrative
- portfolio example
- promotion or role-readiness evidence

#### Repository Hygiene Review

Review the repository structure and content quality.

Check:
- file names
- folder fit
- broken or duplicated concepts
- outdated notes
- sensitive content risk
- files that should be archived
- files that should be promoted into templates or frameworks

Useful outputs:
- cleaned repository
- archived files
- improved naming
- consolidated frameworks
- updated README files where genuinely needed

#### North Star Alignment Review

Assess whether the system is still aligned to the Strategic OS North Star.

Ask:
- Did the system help create better strategic decisions?
- Did it improve stakeholder alignment?
- Did it increase strategic opportunities?
- Did it help turn complexity into useful structure?
- Did it strengthen the desired reputation?
- What should be emphasised next quarter?

Useful outputs:
- quarterly reflection
- strategic direction note
- next-quarter priorities
- system improvement decision

## Cadence Summary

| Rhythm | Main Focus | Primary Function |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Daily | Focus | Clarify the day’s priorities, decisions and follow-ups |
| Weekly | Sensemaking | Convert activity into patterns, actions and alignment |
| Monthly | Opportunity | Develop strategic opportunities, evidence and reusable knowledge |
| Quarterly | Quality | Review system health, agent performance and North Star alignment |

### Daily Prompt

What matters most today, what decisions need attention, and what must not be lost?

### Weekly Prompt

What patterns emerged this week, what moved forward, and what needs to be reframed?

### Monthly Prompt

What opportunities, evidence, lessons and reusable frameworks emerged this month?

### Quarterly Prompt

Is Strategic OS helping create better strategic decisions and more strategic opportunities?

## Success Measures

The operating cadence is working when:

- priorities are clearer
- decisions are easier to frame
- follow-ups are less likely to be missed
- stakeholder alignment is more visible
- lessons are captured and reused
- frameworks improve through use
- career evidence accumulates over time
- strategic opportunities are identified earlier
- the repository remains clean and useful
- agents support judgement without replacing it

## Failure Modes

The cadence is not working if:

- reviews become performative
- files are created but not reused
- daily notes overwhelm the system
- sensitive material is stored unnecessarily
- agents produce output without decisions
- the repository becomes a dumping ground
- strategic opportunities are not reviewed
- lessons are captured but not applied
- the system adds work without improving clarity

## Usage Guidance

Use the cadence lightly.

The goal is not to complete every review perfectly.

The goal is to create enough rhythm that Strategic OS remains useful, current and connected to real work.

When workload is high, reduce the cadence to the essential questions:

- What matters?
- What decision is needed?
- What evidence do I have?
- Who needs to be aligned?
- What should be captured?
- What should be done next?
