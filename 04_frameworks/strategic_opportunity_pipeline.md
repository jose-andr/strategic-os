# Strategic Opportunity Pipeline

## Purpose

This framework defines how Strategic OS captures, shapes, reviews and converts strategic opportunities.

It helps ensure promising signals do not stay as vague ideas, and low-value ideas do not create unnecessary clutter.

The pipeline supports the Strategic OS North Star:

> Increase strategic opportunities by helping leaders make better strategic decisions.

## Core Principle

A strategic opportunity is not just an interesting idea.

A strategic opportunity is a possible path that could improve decision quality, increase influence, create public or organisational value, strengthen capability, build career evidence or open a future option.

The pipeline should help the user decide whether to:

* capture
* shape
* socialise
* activate
* park
* decline
* convert to project
* convert to career evidence
* convert to decision brief
* convert to lesson learned

## What Counts As A Strategic Opportunity

A strategic opportunity may come from:

* customer insight
* service friction
* stakeholder signal
* project ambiguity
* operating model gap
* emerging technology
* organisational pressure
* leadership priority
* career development signal
* capability gap
* data or analytics pattern
* thought leadership idea
* repeated lesson learned
* decision brief implication
* Chief of Staff operating review
* Domain Analytical Agent insight

An opportunity should have at least one of these forms of value:

* strategic value
* customer or public value
* organisational value
* stakeholder value
* capability value
* career evidence value
* learning value
* future optionality value

## Opportunity States

Use these states consistently.

| State                        | Meaning                                                  | Typical Next Action                  |
| ---------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------ |
| Captured                     | The opportunity has been noticed but not yet shaped      | Clarify why it may matter            |
| Shaping                      | The opportunity is being explored, framed or validated   | Use Opportunity Review               |
| Socialising                  | The opportunity needs feedback, sponsorship or alignment | Use Stakeholder Journey Agent        |
| Active                       | The opportunity is being pursued                         | Convert to project or decision brief |
| Parked                       | The opportunity is worth revisiting but not now          | Define revisit trigger               |
| Declined                     | The opportunity is not worth pursuing                    | Capture lesson if useful             |
| Converted to project         | The opportunity now requires delivery structure          | Move to `08_projects/`               |
| Converted to career evidence | The opportunity has created career proof                 | Move to `01_career/`                 |
| Converted to decision brief  | The opportunity requires a decision                      | Move to `03_decision_briefs/`        |
| Converted to lesson learned  | The opportunity created reusable learning                | Move to `05_lessons_learned/`        |

## Pipeline Flow

### 1. Capture

Capture the signal quickly.

Minimum capture fields:

* opportunity title
* source or trigger
* why it may matter
* possible value
* uncertainty
* suggested next step

Suggested location:

`02_strategic_opportunities/index.md`

Do not overwork the opportunity at capture stage.

### 2. Shape

Use shaping when the opportunity appears valuable but unclear.

Clarify:

* what the opportunity is
* who it matters to
* what value it could create
* what evidence exists
* what assumptions need testing
* what trade-offs exist
* what the smallest useful next action is

Recommended template:

`10_templates/opportunity_review.md`

Recommended agent:

Career Architect, Sensemaking Agent or Chief of Staff Agent depending on the source of the opportunity.

### 3. Socialise

Use socialising when the opportunity needs input, support, sponsorship or alignment.

Clarify:

* who needs to understand it
* who can shape it
* who may be affected
* who may sponsor it
* what concerns need to be tested
* what question should be asked
* what should not be overcommitted yet

Recommended template:

`10_templates/stakeholder_alignment_assessment.md`

Recommended agent:

Stakeholder Journey Agent

### 4. Activate

Use activation when the opportunity has enough value, support and clarity to become work.

Convert the opportunity into one of:

* project
* decision brief
* shipping recommendation
* career evidence
* thought leadership draft
* lesson learned
* analytical domain work

Activation should create a concrete next action, owner and storage location.

### 5. Park Or Decline

Not every opportunity should move forward.

Park when:

* value is plausible but timing is wrong
* evidence is insufficient
* capacity is not available
* sponsorship is unclear
* the opportunity depends on another event
* the user wants to preserve the option

Decline when:

* value is low
* trade-offs are too high
* evidence is weak
* the opportunity distracts from stronger priorities
* it does not support the North Star
* it creates more maintenance burden than strategic value

## Opportunity Review Criteria

Assess opportunities against these criteria.

| Criteria                 | Assessment Question                                              |
| ------------------------ | ---------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Strategic value          | Does this increase strategic opportunity or decision quality?    |
| Customer or public value | Could this improve customer, community or public outcomes?       |
| Organisational value     | Could this improve capability, service, efficiency or alignment? |
| Stakeholder value        | Could this improve trust, influence or alignment?                |
| Career value             | Could this create credible evidence or positioning value?        |
| Timing                   | Is now the right time to act, shape or park?                     |
| Evidence strength        | Is the opportunity grounded in real signals?                     |
| Trade-off                | What would this displace?                                        |
| Reversibility            | Can this be tested or shaped without major commitment?           |
| Risk                     | What could go wrong if pursued or ignored?                       |

## Conversion Rules

### Convert To Project

Convert to `08_projects/` when the opportunity requires:

* ongoing delivery
* multiple artefacts
* stakeholder coordination
* decisions over time
* outputs or implementation

### Convert To Decision Brief

Convert to `03_decision_briefs/` when the opportunity requires:

* approval
* prioritisation
* trade-off decision
* sponsor endorsement
* risk acceptance
* formal recommendation

### Convert To Career Evidence

Convert to `01_career/` when the opportunity creates:

* achievement evidence
* feedback evidence
* capability evidence
* promotion evidence
* interview example
* role application proof point

### Convert To Lesson Learned

Convert to `05_lessons_learned/` when the opportunity creates reusable learning about:

* decision-making
* stakeholder engagement
* shipping discipline
* career strategy
* analytics interpretation
* operating rhythm
* service or system design

### Convert To Analytics Domain Work

Convert to `11_analytics/[domain]/` when the opportunity requires:

* governed data interpretation
* source rules
* metric caveats
* Databricks / Genie analysis
* data quality review
* analytical decision support

## Evidence Standards

For every shaped opportunity, separate:

* evidence
* assumptions
* interpretation
* recommendation
* action

Use evidence strength labels:

* Strong: repeated signal, reliable source or observed impact
* Medium: credible but incomplete signal
* Weak: early, anecdotal or uncertain signal

Weak evidence does not mean the opportunity is invalid.

It means the next step should be learning, testing or socialising rather than committing.

## Review Cadence

### Weekly

Use the weekly operating review to identify new opportunity signals and decide whether they should be captured.

### Monthly

Review active, shaping and socialising opportunities.

Ask:

* What should move forward?
* What should be parked?
* What should be declined?
* What needs stakeholder input?
* What needs a decision brief?
* What has become career evidence?

### Quarterly

Use the quarterly Strategic OS assessment to review whether the opportunity pipeline is creating real value.

Ask:

* Were opportunities captured early?
* Were low-value ideas parked or declined?
* Were opportunities converted into decisions, projects or evidence?
* Did the pipeline support the North Star?
* Did it create clutter?

## Privacy And Sensitivity

Do not store:

* raw organisational source data
* customer-level records
* confidential extracts
* sensitive stakeholder commentary
* private HR material
* governed datasets
* unreviewed assumptions about people or teams

Use privacy-safe summaries.

If an opportunity is sensitive, store the minimum safe abstraction and keep detailed context outside the repo.

## Review Checklist

Before advancing an opportunity, check:

* Is the opportunity clearly described?
* Is the value specific?
* Is evidence separated from assumptions?
* Are trade-offs visible?
* Is the next action small and practical?
* Is stakeholder sensitivity handled carefully?
* Is career value grounded rather than inflated?
* Is this worth storing?
* Is the state current?
* Is there a review date?
* Does this support the Strategic OS North Star?

## Failure Modes

The pipeline is not working if:

* every idea becomes an active opportunity
* weak signals are overcommitted too early
* opportunities are captured but never reviewed
* low-value ideas create clutter
* career value is inflated
* stakeholder sensitivity is missed
* opportunities are not converted into action
* parked items have no revisit trigger
* declined items keep reappearing
* the pipeline becomes administration rather than strategic leverage

## Success Measures

The pipeline is working when:

* promising signals are captured early
* opportunities are reviewed at the right cadence
* low-value ideas are parked or declined
* high-value opportunities become projects, decisions or evidence
* stakeholder alignment improves
* career evidence becomes easier to assemble
* strategic judgement improves
* the system creates more useful options over time
