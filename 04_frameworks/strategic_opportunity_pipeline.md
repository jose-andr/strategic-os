# Strategic Opportunity Pipeline

## Purpose

This framework defines how Strategic OS captures, assesses, shapes and converts strategic opportunities.

A strategic opportunity is any signal, relationship, project, decision moment, role possibility, capability gap or emerging need that could help advance the Strategic OS North Star:

> Increase strategic opportunities by helping leaders make better strategic decisions.

The pipeline helps make opportunities visible before they become urgent or disappear.

It supports deliberate action rather than passive waiting.

## Core Principle

Opportunities should be captured early, shaped deliberately and converted only when there is enough value, timing and evidence.

Not every opportunity should be pursued.

A healthy pipeline includes opportunities that are active, parked, declined and converted.

## What Counts As A Strategic Opportunity

A strategic opportunity may include:

- a potential project
- a decision that needs better framing
- a stakeholder relationship to strengthen
- a chance to influence strategy
- a service improvement opening
- a systems mapping opportunity
- a business design opportunity
- a customer insight worth developing
- a leadership or promotion evidence moment
- a thought leadership idea
- a new role or secondment possibility
- an analytics-enabled decision opportunity
- a reusable framework or template opportunity
- an emerging organisational pressure point

## What Does Not Belong In The Pipeline

Do not use the pipeline for:

- routine tasks
- low-value ideas
- private sensitive matters
- raw customer records
- unfiltered meeting notes
- confidential organisational source material
- vague ambitions with no possible next action
- opportunities that cannot be safely stored

Use `00_inbox/` first when something is still unclear.

## Opportunity States

Use these states consistently.

| State | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| Captured | Noted as a possible opportunity, but not yet assessed |
| Shaping | Being clarified, framed or developed |
| Socialising | Being discussed with relevant people |
| Active | Currently being pursued |
| Parked | Useful, but not right for now |
| Declined | Intentionally not pursued |
| Converted to project | Moved into `08_projects/` |
| Converted to career evidence | Moved into `01_career/` |
| Converted to thought leadership | Moved into `09_thought_leadership/` |
| Converted to decision brief | Moved into `03_decision_briefs/` |
| Converted to framework | Moved into `04_frameworks/` |
| Converted to lesson learned | Moved into `05_lessons_learned/` |

## Pipeline Flow

The standard flow is:

1. Capture signal
2. Assess strategic value
3. Shape the opportunity
4. Socialise where appropriate
5. Decide whether to pursue
6. Convert to the right output
7. Review and maintain

## 1. Capture Signal

### Purpose

Capture opportunity signals before they are lost.

Possible sources include:

- meetings
- stakeholder comments
- executive priorities
- customer pain points
- project tensions
- service data
- analytics observations
- external trends
- role descriptions
- feedback
- repeated organisational patterns
- unresolved decisions
- capability gaps

### Default Capture Location

Use:

`00_inbox/`

Move to:

`02_strategic_opportunities/index.md`

when the signal has enough value to track.

### Capture Questions

Ask:

- What is the opportunity?
- Why might it matter?
- Who may benefit?
- What decision or outcome could it influence?
- What evidence exists?
- What is still uncertain?
- What is the smallest next action?

## 2. Assess Strategic Value

### Purpose

Assess whether the opportunity is worth shaping.

Use the following dimensions.

## Strategic Value

Does this opportunity help leaders make better strategic decisions?

Consider:

- decision quality
- strategic clarity
- organisational alignment
- future readiness
- ability to reduce ambiguity
- ability to create better choices

## Stakeholder Value

Does this opportunity help stakeholders align, understand or act?

Consider:

- trust
- shared framing
- influence
- engagement
- readiness
- communication clarity
- decision ownership

## Organisational Value

Does this opportunity help the organisation improve?

Consider:

- service quality
- operational feasibility
- risk reduction
- capability uplift
- cost-to-serve insight
- delivery confidence
- clearer governance

## Customer Value

Does this opportunity improve outcomes for people using or affected by services?

Consider:

- effort
- access
- fairness
- clarity
- speed
- continuity
- confidence
- ability to complete tasks

## Career Value

Does this opportunity create evidence of strategic contribution?

Consider:

- systems thinking
- business design practice
- human-centred design practice
- AI-enabled capability
- stakeholder influence
- decision support
- delivery and follow-through
- strategic reputation

## Evidence Value

Does this opportunity create reusable learning or proof?

Consider:

- measurable impact
- quality of examples
- transferability
- credibility
- ability to support a future narrative
- ability to become a framework, template or case study

## Timing

Is now the right time?

Consider:

- decision window
- stakeholder readiness
- organisational momentum
- workload
- dependency risk
- whether the opportunity should be active, shaped or parked

## Trade-Offs

What could this opportunity cost?

Consider:

- time
- focus
- stakeholder attention
- reputational risk
- complexity
- competing priorities
- risk of over-investing too early

## 3. Shape The Opportunity

### Purpose

Shaping turns a raw signal into a clearer opportunity.

A shaped opportunity should include:

- opportunity statement
- strategic value
- audience or stakeholders
- evidence
- assumptions
- risks
- next action
- possible output path

### Shaping Questions

Ask:

- What is the opportunity really about?
- What problem or decision does it connect to?
- What would make this valuable?
- What evidence would increase confidence?
- Who needs to understand or support it?
- What is the smallest credible next step?
- What output should this become?

## 4. Socialise Where Appropriate

### Purpose

Some opportunities need conversation before commitment.

Socialising can test whether the opportunity is real, useful and timely.

Use socialising when:

- stakeholder support matters
- timing is uncertain
- the opportunity needs sponsorship
- the framing may be sensitive
- evidence is incomplete
- ownership is unclear

### Socialising Questions

Ask:

- Who should hear this first?
- What framing will make sense to them?
- What concern might they have?
- What should be tested in conversation?
- What should not be overclaimed?
- What would indicate readiness?

## 5. Decide Whether To Pursue

### Purpose

Not every opportunity deserves action.

Use the opportunity states to make a clear choice.

## Pursue

Move to `Active` when:

- value is clear
- timing is right
- the next action is known
- there is enough evidence or stakeholder interest
- the opportunity connects to the North Star

## Shape Further

Keep in `Shaping` when:

- value seems promising
- evidence is incomplete
- framing is still unclear
- stakeholders need to be understood
- timing is not yet certain

## Socialise

Move to `Socialising` when:

- stakeholder validation is needed
- sponsorship is uncertain
- framing needs testing
- there is a possible decision window

## Park

Move to `Parked` when:

- the idea has value
- timing is wrong
- capacity is limited
- evidence is too weak
- there is no immediate path to action

## Decline

Move to `Declined` when:

- strategic value is low
- risks outweigh benefits
- the opportunity does not support the North Star
- it is not safe or appropriate to pursue
- it creates distraction from higher-value work

## Convert

Move to a converted state when the opportunity has become a concrete output.

## 6. Convert To The Right Output

### Purpose

Mature opportunities should become useful artefacts, not remain in the pipeline indefinitely.

| Opportunity Type | Conversion Path |
| --- | --- |
| Decision opportunity | `03_decision_briefs/` |
| Project opportunity | `08_projects/` |
| Career evidence | `01_career/` |
| Thought leadership idea | `09_thought_leadership/` |
| Reusable method | `04_frameworks/` |
| Reusable output structure | `10_templates/` |
| Stakeholder pattern | `06_stakeholder_patterns/` |
| Lesson learned | `05_lessons_learned/` |
| Analytics opportunity | `11_analytics/` |

## 7. Review And Maintain

### Monthly Review

Review the opportunity pipeline monthly.

Ask:

- What new opportunities emerged?
- What moved forward?
- What is stuck?
- What should be socialised?
- What should be parked?
- What should be declined?
- What should be converted?
- What opportunity needs evidence?

### Quarterly Review

Review the pipeline during quarterly Strategic OS assessment.

Ask:

- Did opportunities become action?
- Did opportunities improve decision quality?
- Did opportunities strengthen stakeholder alignment?
- Did opportunities create career evidence?
- Did opportunities create reusable knowledge?
- Is the pipeline too full?
- Are low-value opportunities being removed?

## Quality Bar

A strong opportunity entry should be:

- clearly named
- connected to the North Star
- specific enough to act on
- explicit about uncertainty
- privacy-safe
- linked to a possible output
- reviewed at an appropriate cadence
- either moved forward, parked, declined or converted

## Privacy And Governance Rules

Do not store:

- confidential stakeholder names
- raw organisational documents
- customer-level records
- private employee matters
- governed datasets
- sensitive internal issues
- raw email extracts
- unresolved sensitive risks

Prefer storing:

- safe summaries
- abstracted opportunity labels
- reusable patterns
- decision logic
- non-sensitive evidence
- next actions
- career-safe proof points

## Success Measures

The opportunity pipeline is working when:

- promising opportunities are not lost
- strategic value is visible
- next actions are clear
- opportunities are reviewed monthly
- low-value opportunities are parked or declined
- mature opportunities convert into outputs
- career evidence accumulates naturally
- stakeholder alignment opportunities are easier to see
- decisions are shaped earlier
- the system creates more strategic opportunities over time

## Failure Modes

The pipeline is failing if:

- every idea becomes an opportunity
- opportunities have no next action
- the index becomes stale
- sensitive details are stored unnecessarily
- low-value opportunities remain active
- opportunities are never converted
- career evidence is not captured
- strategic value is unclear
- review becomes performative
- the pipeline becomes a task list

## Minimal Use Rule

Use the pipeline lightly.

Capture what matters.

Shape what has promise.

Pursue what has value and timing.

Decline what distracts.

Convert what matures.
