# Strategic OS QA Model

## Purpose

This framework defines how to assess the quality, usefulness and safety of Strategic OS over time.

Strategic OS should not only contain files.

It should help create better decisions, stronger stakeholder alignment, reusable knowledge, career evidence and strategic opportunities.

The QA model provides a repeatable way to review whether the system is working.

## North Star

Strategic OS should be assessed against the North Star:

> Increase strategic opportunities by helping leaders make better strategic decisions.

## Core Principle

Assess the system by the value it creates, not the volume of files it contains.

A healthy Strategic OS should make important work clearer, safer, easier to reuse and more decision-ready.

## QA Review Rhythm

Use this model during quarterly Strategic OS review.

It may also be used after major project phases, repo restructuring, agent changes or analytics-domain updates.

Recommended cadence:

| Rhythm | Use |
| --- | --- |
| Monthly | Light review of opportunities, lessons, career evidence and templates |
| Quarterly | Full Strategic OS QA assessment |
| After major project | Targeted project and evidence review |
| After agent change | Agent performance and boundary review |
| After analytics change | Source, caveat and reliability review |

## QA Dimensions

Strategic OS should be assessed across ten dimensions:

1. Strategic usefulness
2. Decision quality
3. Stakeholder alignment support
4. Shipping discipline
5. Career development support
6. Agent output quality
7. Template usefulness
8. Repository hygiene
9. Privacy and governance compliance
10. Cross-tool and analytics reliability

## 1. Strategic Usefulness

### Purpose

Assess whether Strategic OS is helping create or shape strategic opportunities.

### Review Questions

- Did Strategic OS help identify strategic opportunities?
- Did it help clarify why an opportunity mattered?
- Did it help connect day-to-day work to strategic value?
- Did it help make complexity visible?
- Did it support better options, framing or recommendations?
- Did it strengthen the desired reputation?

Desired reputation:

> He helps us make better strategic decisions.

### Evidence To Look For

- strategic opportunities captured
- opportunity reviews created
- opportunities converted into projects or evidence
- decision briefs used
- frameworks reused
- recommendations adopted
- thought leadership seeds developed
- stakeholder conversations improved

### Quality Signals

Strong signals:

- opportunities are visible and reviewed
- outputs support real decisions
- strategic value is clear
- the system creates useful options
- work connects to organisational or customer outcomes

Weak signals:

- opportunities are captured but not reviewed
- files exist without strategic use
- outputs are descriptive but not decision-oriented
- the system stores information without creating leverage

## 2. Decision Quality

### Purpose

Assess whether Strategic OS improves decision framing and decision readiness.

### Review Questions

- Are decisions easier to frame?
- Are options clear?
- Are trade-offs visible?
- Are assumptions separated from evidence?
- Are risks and caveats named?
- Are recommendations practical?
- Is the next action clear?
- Is the decision owner or audience clear?

### Evidence To Look For

- decision briefs
- problem framing notes
- trade-off summaries
- options assessments
- recommendations
- decision records in project folders
- lessons from decisions

### Quality Signals

Strong signals:

- decisions include context, options, trade-offs and recommendations
- uncertainty is explicit
- next actions are clear
- decision outputs are reused
- recommendations are adopted, adapted or discussed

Weak signals:

- decisions are buried in notes
- recommendations are vague
- assumptions are hidden
- trade-offs are missing
- decision outputs do not lead to action

## 3. Stakeholder Alignment Support

### Purpose

Assess whether Strategic OS helps improve stakeholder understanding, alignment and influence.

### Review Questions

- Did the system help identify stakeholder alignment risks?
- Did it help prepare better conversations?
- Did it make stakeholder needs or concerns clearer?
- Did it support trust, framing or shared understanding?
- Were stakeholder patterns captured safely?
- Were sensitive details abstracted appropriately?

### Evidence To Look For

- stakeholder alignment assessments
- stakeholder preparation notes
- project stakeholder notes
- reusable stakeholder patterns
- communication framing notes
- meeting preparation outputs

### Quality Signals

Strong signals:

- stakeholder risks are surfaced early
- engagement framing improves
- alignment conditions are visible
- notes are privacy-safe
- patterns are reusable across work

Weak signals:

- stakeholder context is missing
- private commentary is stored
- influence risks are discovered too late
- notes describe people but not alignment conditions
- recommendations ignore stakeholder reality

## 4. Shipping Discipline

### Purpose

Assess whether Strategic OS helps move useful work forward rather than over-refining it.

### Review Questions

- Did work move from thinking to output?
- Were unfinished drafts shipped, socialised, refined or stopped?
- Did the system reduce over-analysis?
- Were next actions clear?
- Were project folders used appropriately?
- Were lessons captured after shipping?

### Evidence To Look For

- shipping recommendations
- project outputs
- completed templates
- shipped artefacts
- lessons learned
- project closure notes
- archived drafts

### Quality Signals

Strong signals:

- outputs are completed and used
- drafts do not accumulate indefinitely
- work is scoped to the smallest useful next step
- lessons are captured after delivery
- project folders remain navigable

Weak signals:

- many drafts, few outputs
- over-structured work
- unclear next actions
- abandoned project folders
- no lessons captured after delivery

## 5. Career Development Support

### Purpose

Assess whether Strategic OS helps convert work into credible career evidence and opportunity positioning.

### Review Questions

- Was career evidence captured close to the work?
- Are achievements outcome-oriented?
- Are capability examples clear?
- Is promotion readiness easier to assess?
- Are opportunities connected to evidence?
- Is evidence safe to retain?
- Does the evidence support the desired reputation?

### Evidence To Look For

- achievements
- feedback summaries
- opportunity reviews
- promotion readiness assessments
- portfolio-safe examples
- career guidance outputs
- capability narratives

### Quality Signals

Strong signals:

- evidence is specific and outcome-oriented
- examples connect to capability
- strategic influence is visible
- career stories are easier to prepare
- promotion readiness gaps are clear

Weak signals:

- evidence is captured too late
- files describe activity but not impact
- sensitive details are retained
- career material is disconnected from real work
- capability claims lack proof

## 6. Agent Output Quality

### Purpose

Assess whether Strategic OS agents are producing useful, bounded and reviewable outputs.

### Review Questions

- Was the right agent used for the right task?
- Did the output follow the agent spec?
- Did the agent stay within autonomy boundaries?
- Was human review required and respected?
- Did the agent separate evidence, assumptions and recommendations?
- Did the agent produce useful structure rather than noise?
- Did the agent create unnecessary work?

### Evidence To Look For

- agent outputs
- daily briefings
- decision briefs
- stakeholder assessments
- shipping recommendations
- career guidance
- agent review notes
- escalation notes

### Quality Signals

Strong signals:

- outputs are concise and useful
- agent role boundaries are clear
- human-in-the-loop points are respected
- outputs support judgement
- recommendations include trade-offs and uncertainty

Weak signals:

- agents overstep into decisions
- outputs are generic
- assumptions are presented as facts
- agent outputs are not reviewed
- agents create more work than clarity

## 7. Template Usefulness

### Purpose

Assess whether templates reduce friction and improve output consistency.

### Review Questions

- Are templates being used?
- Do templates make outputs easier to create?
- Are templates practical and not too heavy?
- Do templates match agent outputs?
- Are review checklists useful?
- Are any templates missing?
- Should any templates be simplified, merged or retired?

### Evidence To Look For

- completed template outputs
- repeated use of templates
- template gaps
- user friction
- template revisions
- outputs that required custom structures

### Quality Signals

Strong signals:

- templates save time
- outputs are consistent
- review sections improve quality
- templates match real workflows
- templates are reused across contexts

Weak signals:

- templates are too long
- templates are not used
- outputs ignore template structure
- templates duplicate each other
- templates create administration without value

## 8. Repository Hygiene

### Purpose

Assess whether Strategic OS remains easy to navigate, maintain and reuse.

### Review Questions

- Are files easy to find?
- Are file names consistent?
- Are indexes maintained where useful?
- Are outdated files archived or marked?
- Are folders being used correctly?
- Are project folders becoming dumping grounds?
- Are duplicates consolidated?
- Are temporary files removed?

### Evidence To Look For

- folder structure
- file names
- indexes
- archived files
- duplicated files
- stale drafts
- project folders
- README consistency, reviewed only during end-of-day or planned documentation passes

### Quality Signals

Strong signals:

- folder use is clear
- file names are consistent
- indexes are lightweight and useful
- outdated material is marked or archived
- files are reusable without chat context

Weak signals:

- files are hard to find
- drafts accumulate
- naming is inconsistent
- indexes are stale
- folder boundaries are unclear
- README drift is left unresolved at closeout

## 9. Privacy And Governance Compliance

### Purpose

Assess whether Strategic OS remains safe to maintain and does not duplicate official records or sensitive source material.

### Review Questions

- Is raw organisational source data excluded?
- Are customer-level records excluded?
- Are confidential extracts excluded?
- Are sensitive stakeholder details abstracted?
- Are private HR or employee matters avoided?
- Are assumptions marked clearly?
- Are analytics caveats included?
- Are files safe to commit?

### Evidence To Look For

- project files
- career evidence
- stakeholder notes
- analytics notes
- decision briefs
- source references
- file names
- indexes

### Quality Signals

Strong signals:

- files use safe summaries
- sensitive details are abstracted
- source systems remain source of truth
- assumptions are visible
- privacy notes are used where helpful

Weak signals:

- confidential extracts appear in files
- customer details are retained
- private stakeholder commentary is stored
- raw operational data is copied into GitHub
- assumptions are presented as facts

## 10. Cross-Tool And Analytics Reliability

### Purpose

Assess whether Strategic OS respects tool boundaries and uses analytics sources responsibly.

### Review Questions

- Are source-of-truth boundaries clear?
- Is GitHub storing reusable knowledge rather than official records?
- Are Databricks / Genie caveats preserved?
- Are Power BI dashboards treated as reporting sources of truth?
- Are SharePoint / OneDrive documents left in official storage?
- Are Teams, email and calendar summaries privacy-safe?
- Are analytics domains documented clearly?
- Are contextual signals labelled correctly?

### Evidence To Look For

- cross-tool architecture notes
- analytics domain files
- source rules
- metric caveats
- Genie context
- agent consistency checks
- analytics interpretation notes
- references to official systems

### Quality Signals

Strong signals:

- tool roles are clear
- governed data stays in governed systems
- analytics caveats are visible
- domain-agent patterns are documented
- Strategic OS stores interpretation, not raw data

Weak signals:

- GitHub becomes a shadow record system
- Databricks outputs are copied without caveats
- Power BI screenshots replace governed reporting
- official documents are duplicated
- tool boundaries are unclear

## Overall Health Rating

Use this overall rating scale:

| Rating | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| Strong | The system is useful, safe, current and creating strategic value |
| Working | The system is generally useful, with minor improvements needed |
| Needs attention | The system has value but requires clean-up or stronger discipline |
| Not working | The system is creating clutter, risk or little strategic value |

Use these questions during quarterly QA.

- What recommendations were adopted or discussed?
- Where did Strategic OS improve clarity or alignment?

- Which outputs were most useful?
- Which outputs were too heavy or unclear?
- Which outputs were reused?
- Which outputs led to action?

### Evidence Quality

- Where was evidence strong?
- Where were assumptions too weak?
- Were caveats visible?
- Were contextual signals labelled correctly?

- Which agents were useful?
- Which agents need clearer boundaries?
- Which agents created unnecessary work?

- Was the inbox triaged?
- Were opportunities reviewed?
- Were lessons captured?

- Did daily work connect back to the North Star?

## Success Measures

The QA model is working when:

- quarterly review is easier
- system value is visible
- improvement actions are clear
- weak areas are identified early
- agents and templates improve over time
- privacy and governance risks are reduced
- strategic opportunities are easier to track
- career evidence is easier to assemble
- the repository remains useful and safe

## Failure Modes

The QA model is failing if:

- review becomes performative
- everything is rated positively without evidence
- improvement actions are not followed up
- sensitive material is ignored
- poor agent outputs are not addressed
- stale templates remain in use
- repository clutter is normalised
- analytics caveats are lost
- the North Star is not used as the anchor

## Minimal Use Rule

Use the QA model to improve the system, not to create bureaucracy.

The review should produce three things:

- a clear view of what is working
- a short list of what needs improvement
- a decision about what to do next
