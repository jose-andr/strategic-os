# Agent Review Checklist

## Purpose

Use this checklist to review outputs created with Strategic OS agents before they are stored, shared or acted on.

The checklist helps keep agent outputs clear, useful, evidence-aware, privacy-aware and aligned to the Strategic OS purpose.

Agents support the Strategic OS North Star:

> Increase strategic opportunities by helping leaders make better strategic decisions.

Agent outputs do not replace human judgement, stakeholder engagement, governance or final decision-making.

## When to use

Use this checklist before:

* sharing a decision brief
* acting on a stakeholder alignment assessment
* following a shipping recommendation
* using career guidance
* saving an opportunity review
* using a daily briefing to guide priorities
* turning an agent output into a project artefact
* reusing an agent output in organisational work
* storing an output as durable Strategic OS knowledge

## Core review questions

### 1. Decision clarity

* Is the decision, action or judgement clear?
* Is the output structured around what needs to happen next?
* Is the recommendation practical?
* Is the next step specific enough to act on?
* Is the owner or intended audience clear?
* Is the output clear about whether it is advice, preparation, recommendation or action support?

### 2. Evidence quality

* Is evidence separated from interpretation?
* Are assumptions clearly marked?
* Are unsupported claims removed or caveated?
* Are source limitations visible?
* Is contextual evidence clearly distinguished from governed reporting?
* Is the output clear about what is known, inferred, missing or uncertain?
* Has the agent avoided inventing evidence, impact, stakeholder views or commitments?

### 3. Human judgement

* Is it clear where human judgement is required?
* Are sensitive interpretations checked before use?
* Are stakeholder assumptions treated carefully?
* Are governance or approval points visible?
* Does the output avoid making commitments on behalf of the user?
* Does the output avoid treating draft advice as approved advice?
* Has the output flagged any Level 5 human approval risks?

### 4. Strategic alignment

* Does the output support the Strategic OS North Star?
* Does it help leaders make better strategic decisions?
* Does it connect customer outcomes and organisational outcomes where relevant?
* Does it strengthen the desired reputation?
* Does it create or protect strategic opportunity?
* Does it reduce decision friction, stakeholder friction or operational drag?

### 5. Practical usefulness

* Is the output concise enough to use?
* Is the language clear and plain?
* Are trade-offs visible?
* Are risks and dependencies named?
* Is the recommendation proportionate to the problem?
* Does the output make the next useful action easier?
* Does the output avoid creating unnecessary extra work?
* Is the output durable enough to keep, or should it remain a temporary working note?

### 6. Privacy and portability

* Does the output avoid storing raw organisational source data?
* Is sensitive information removed or generalised?
* Is customer-level, staff-level or stakeholder-sensitive detail handled safely?
* Is the knowledge portable beyond one employer context?
* Is the file safe to keep in Strategic OS?
* Does the output avoid copying confidential extracts from systems of record?
* Is the proposed storage location appropriate?

### 7. Reuse value

* Could this become a reusable pattern, template, lesson or case study?
* Should a lesson learned be created?
* Should the output be linked to an existing framework?
* Should a future prompt or agent rule be improved based on this?
* Could this become career evidence?
* Could this become a stakeholder pattern?
* Could this become a strategic opportunity?
* Should the material be indexed?

## Agent-specific checks

### Sensemaking Agent

* Is the problem framed clearly?
* Are options compared?
* Are trade-offs visible?
* Is the recommendation decision-ready?
* Are evidence, assumptions, interpretation and recommendation separated?
* Is ambiguity reduced without creating false certainty?
* Is the decision owner or next decision point clear?

### Stakeholder Journey Agent

* Are stakeholders grouped accurately?
* Is resistance described fairly?
* Are influence pathways clear?
* Is the next conversation obvious?
* Are stakeholder assumptions clearly marked?
* Is sensitive stakeholder commentary removed or generalised?
* Does the engagement sequence protect trust?

### Shipping Coach

* Is the recommendation clearly Ship, Socialise, Refine or Stop?
* Is the minimum next action clear?
* Is the risk of waiting visible?
* Is the quality threshold realistic?
* Are genuine blockers separated from optional polish?
* Are stakeholder, governance, privacy or reputational risks visible?
* Does the output avoid pushing speed over trust?

### Career Architect

* Does the output support long-term positioning?
* Does it create or strengthen career evidence?
* Are trade-offs visible?
* Is the recommendation grounded in real proof points?
* Are achievements stated without exaggeration?
* Are capability gaps named clearly?
* Is sensitive organisational detail removed before reuse?

### Chief of Staff Agent

* Does the output clarify what matters today?
* Are priorities limited and realistic?
* Are tasks, decisions, risks, dependencies and reminders separated?
* Are decision-required items visible?
* Are follow-ups specific and attributable?
* Are meetings or stakeholder interactions prepared with a clear purpose?
* Are assumptions separated from confirmed commitments?
* Does the output protect focus rather than create more work?
* Are sensitive items, escalations and approval points clearly flagged?
* Does the output avoid making commitments or sending communication on behalf of the user?
* Is ephemeral daily material kept out of durable storage unless it creates reusable value, decision context, evidence or a follow-up obligation?

### Domain Analytical Agent

Use this section for future Databricks / Genie or analytics-domain agents.

* Is the analytical domain clear?
* Are source rules and metric caveats visible?
* Is governed reporting separated from exploratory analysis?
* Are data quality limitations stated?
* Are claims proportionate to the available evidence?
* Is the output stored in the relevant `11_analytics/[domain]/` folder?
* Has human review occurred before using the output in governance, reporting or strategic recommendations?
* Are Databricks / Genie outputs treated as decision-support evidence rather than final organisational truth?

## Output storage check

Before storing an output, choose the most appropriate location:

* raw or unprocessed material: `00_inbox/`
* career evidence: `01_career/`
* strategic opportunity: `02_strategic_opportunities/`
* decision brief: `03_decision_briefs/`
* reusable framework: `04_frameworks/`
* lesson learned: `05_lessons_learned/`
* stakeholder pattern: `06_stakeholder_patterns/`
* agent specification or operating rule: `07_ai_agents/`
* project artefact: `08_projects/`
* thought leadership draft: `09_thought_leadership/`
* reusable template: `10_templates/`
* analytics domain file: `11_analytics/[domain]/`

Do not store the output if it is only a temporary working note, duplicates an existing artefact, contains sensitive raw data, or creates maintenance burden without reuse value.

## Final review decision

Choose one:

* Ready to use
* Needs light refinement
* Needs evidence check
* Needs privacy or sensitivity review
* Needs human approval
* Store as reusable knowledge
* Keep as temporary working note
* Do not use yet
