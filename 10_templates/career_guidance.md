# Career Guidance

## Purpose

Use this template to translate current work, feedback, opportunities, goals and uncertainty into practical career development guidance.

It helps the user clarify:

* current career positioning
* desired future positioning
* evidence already available
* evidence still needed
* capability gaps
* opportunities worth pursuing
* relationships to strengthen
* trade-offs to make
* next practical career move

This template supports the Career Architect Agent.

North Star:

> Increase strategic opportunities by helping leaders make better strategic decisions.

## When To Use

Use this template when:

* reviewing career direction
* preparing for promotion
* assessing capability gaps
* translating project work into career evidence
* deciding where to focus effort
* shaping a clearer strategic career narrative
* preparing for a development conversation
* assessing whether an opportunity is worth pursuing
* deciding how to position recent work
* turning feedback into a practical development plan

Do not use this template to inflate achievements, invent impact, or turn confidential organisational context into public career claims.

## Review Checklist

Before acting on this guidance, check:

* Does this support long-term strategic positioning?
* Does this strengthen the desired reputation?
* Is the next move practical?
* Is the guidance supported by evidence?
* Are claims grounded and proportionate?
* Are capability gaps clear?
* Are trade-offs visible?
* Could this create reusable career evidence?
* Is sensitive information removed or generalised?
* Is human review required before use?
* Is the suggested storage location appropriate?

## Output Quality Bar

Useful career guidance should be:

* grounded in real evidence
* clear about positioning
* practical about next steps
* honest about gaps
* specific about capability
* careful with sensitive information
* useful for future applications, promotion or development conversations
* authentic to the user’s actual work and direction

## Failure Modes

This template is not working if:

* it turns activity into inflated achievement
* it invents impact
* it uses generic career language
* it ignores evidence quality
* it hides capability gaps
* it recommends opportunities without trade-offs
* it stores sensitive information unnecessarily
* it makes the user sound less authentic
* it creates career claims that cannot be supported
