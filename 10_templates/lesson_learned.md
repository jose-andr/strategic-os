# Lesson Learned

## Purpose

Use this template to capture reusable learning from work, decisions, experiments, stakeholder engagement, delivery, analysis or reflection.

It helps the user turn experience into Strategic OS knowledge that can improve future decisions, reduce repeated mistakes and strengthen reusable practice.

North Star:

> Increase strategic opportunities by helping leaders make better strategic decisions.

## When To Use

Use this template when something has produced learning worth keeping.

Use it for:

* project learning
* decision learning
* stakeholder engagement learning
* shipping or delivery learning
* career learning
* analytical caveats
* failed assumptions
* repeated patterns
* operating rhythm improvements
* service design or business design insights
* lessons from work that was shipped, stopped, parked or redirected

Do not use this template for:

* raw notes
* venting
* temporary reflections with no reuse value
* confidential source material
* sensitive stakeholder commentary
* customer-level records
* lessons that cannot be safely generalised

## Review Checklist

Before storing or using this lesson, check:

* Is the lesson clear?
* Is it reusable beyond the immediate moment?
* Is evidence separated from interpretation?
* Are assumptions visible?
* Is the lesson proportionate to the evidence?
* Is sensitive information removed or generalised?
* Does it avoid blaming individuals?
* Does it identify how to act differently next time?
* Is the storage location appropriate?
* Does it support better strategic decision-making?

## Output Quality Bar

A useful lesson learned should be:

* specific
* reusable
* evidence-aware
* proportionate
* safe to store
* practical for future decisions
* clear about what should change
* free of unnecessary sensitive detail

## Failure Modes

This template is not working if:

* it becomes a diary entry
* it captures emotion without reusable learning
* it blames people instead of identifying patterns
* it stores sensitive detail unnecessarily
* it overclaims from weak evidence
* it does not say what to do differently
* it duplicates an existing lesson
* it creates maintenance burden without future value
