# Quarterly Strategic OS Assessment

## Purpose

This template supports a quarterly assessment of whether Strategic OS is working.

It helps review strategic usefulness, decision quality, stakeholder alignment, career evidence, agent performance, template usefulness, repository hygiene, privacy and governance, analytics reliability and North Star alignment.

The assessment should produce a clear view of:

- what worked
- what did not work
- what should improve next quarter
- what should stop
- whether Strategic OS is creating strategic value

## When To Use

Use this template once per quarter.

It may also be used after:

- major project phases
- significant repository changes
- new agent creation
- major analytics-domain updates
- major career or opportunity reviews
- Strategic OS quality concerns

## Storage Guidance

Completed quarterly assessments may be stored in one of the following locations:

- `05_lessons_learned/`
- `04_frameworks/qa_assessments/`
- `01_career/promotion_readiness/`

Choose the location based on the primary use of the assessment.

Use `05_lessons_learned/` when the assessment is mainly about system learning.

Use `04_frameworks/qa_assessments/` when the assessment is mainly about system quality.

Use `01_career/promotion_readiness/` when the assessment is being used as career evidence.

## Review Checklist

Before finalising the assessment, check:

- Is the North Star alignment score justified?
- Is the health rating supported by evidence?
- Are strategic opportunities reviewed?
- Are decisions and recommendations captured?
- Is career evidence identified?
- Are agent issues visible?
- Are template issues visible?
- Are privacy and governance risks reviewed?
- Are analytics caveats reviewed where relevant?
- Are improvement actions practical?
- Is anything sensitive removed or abstracted?
- Is the next quarter direction clear?

## Failure Modes

This assessment is not working if:

- everything is rated positively without evidence
- the review becomes performative
- improvement actions are too vague
- privacy risks are ignored
- analytics caveats are skipped
- templates are reviewed without usage evidence
- agents are praised without output quality review
- the assessment does not lead to decisions
- the North Star is not used as the anchor

## Minimal Use Rule

A useful quarterly assessment should produce three things:

- a clear view of what worked
- a short list of what needs to improve
- a decision about what to do next quarter

Keep the review practical.
