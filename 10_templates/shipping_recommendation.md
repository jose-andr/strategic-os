# Shipping Recommendation

## Purpose

Use this template to decide whether a piece of work should be shipped, socialised, refined or stopped.

It helps the user distinguish between:

* work that is ready to move
* work that needs feedback
* work that needs a specific refinement
* work that should be stopped or parked
* useful quality improvement
* avoidable over-refinement
* genuine risk
* hesitation caused by uncertainty

This template supports the Shipping Coach.

North Star:

> Increase strategic opportunities by helping leaders make better strategic decisions.

## When To Use

Use this template when:

* a draft is sitting unfinished
* work is becoming over-refined
* it is unclear whether something is ready to share
* momentum is slowing
* more feedback is needed before further refinement
* a piece of work may no longer be worth continuing
* the next useful action is unclear
* a decision is needed about whether to ship, socialise, refine or stop

Do not use this template to push unsafe, incoherent or sensitive work out before it is ready.

## Review Checklist

Before acting on this recommendation, check:

* Is the intended outcome clear?
* Is the audience clear?
* Is the work coherent enough for the next step?
* Are real blockers separated from optional refinements?
* Is the quality threshold proportionate?
* Is the risk of waiting visible?
* Is the risk of moving now visible?
* Is the recommendation clearly Ship, Socialise, Refine or Stop?
* Is the next action specific?
* Is sensitive information removed or generalised?
* Is human review required before action?
* Does the recommendation protect trust as well as momentum?

## Output Quality Bar

A useful shipping recommendation should be:

* practical
* specific
* honest about risk
* clear about the next action
* clear about the quality threshold
* resistant to perfectionism
* careful with stakeholder and governance risk
* focused on progress, learning or intentional stopping

## Failure Modes

This template is not working if:

* it pushes work out before it is safe or coherent
* it treats all hesitation as perfectionism
* it ignores real quality gaps
* it hides stakeholder or governance risk
* it recommends more refinement without a stop condition
* it creates vague next steps
* it fails to identify the audience
* it treats speed as more important than trust
* it avoids recommending Stop when work is no longer useful
