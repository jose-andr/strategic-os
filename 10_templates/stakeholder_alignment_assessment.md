# Stakeholder Alignment Assessment

## Purpose

Use this template to assess who needs to understand, support, shape or act on a piece of work for it to succeed.

It helps the user clarify:

* who matters and why
* what decision, support or input is needed
* where alignment already exists
* where resistance, uncertainty or silence may affect progress
* how engagement should be sequenced
* what message or ask is appropriate for each audience
* what requires human judgement before action

This template supports the Stakeholder Journey Agent.

North Star:

> Increase strategic opportunities by helping leaders make better strategic decisions.

## When To Use

Use this template when:

* a recommendation needs stakeholder support
* a decision is not progressing
* there are competing perspectives
* a project needs clearer engagement sequencing
* resistance, ambiguity or silence may affect progress
* a message needs to be adapted for different audiences
* decision rights or influence pathways are unclear
* affected groups may not yet be represented
* stakeholder trust or timing matters

Do not use this template to label stakeholders, infer motives without evidence or treat engagement as persuasion.

Store only if the assessment creates durable value, decision context, project continuity, reusable engagement learning or stakeholder pattern evidence.

Do not store:

* sensitive stakeholder commentary
* unreviewed assumptions about people
* raw organisational source data
* confidential extracts
* customer-level records
* temporary engagement notes with no reuse value

## Review Checklist

Before using this assessment, check:

* Is the decision or action needed clear?
* Are stakeholders grouped accurately?
* Are influence, interest, impact and decision rights separated?
* Are assumptions separated from evidence?
* Are confidence levels visible where interpretation is involved?
* Is resistance described carefully and fairly?
* Is the engagement sequence practical?
* Is the next conversation clear?
* Is communication framing respectful and non-manipulative?
* Is sensitive information removed or generalised?
* Does the narrative help people make a better strategic decision?

## Output Quality Bar

A useful stakeholder alignment assessment should be:

* clear about the work or decision
* honest about uncertainty
* fair to stakeholders
* practical about sequencing
* specific about the next conversation
* careful with sensitive interpretation
* grounded in evidence where possible
* useful for decision-making and trust-building

## Failure Modes

This template is not working if:

* it labels stakeholders unfairly
* it infers motives without evidence
* it treats engagement as persuasion
* it creates a map without a next conversation
* it hides trade-offs or risks
* it stores sensitive commentary unnecessarily
* it ignores missing voices
* it treats communication as a substitute for participation
* it makes stakeholder management more important than stakeholder trust
